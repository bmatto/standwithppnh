FAQ

On August 5, 2015 the NH executive council voted to reject $638,000 br in federal Title 10 funding
for health centers in NH operated by br Planned Parenthood of Northern New England.

What is Planned Parenthood?

Planned Parenthood delivers vital reproductive health care, sex education, and information to millions of women, men, and young people worldwide. For nearly 100 years, Planned Parenthood has promoted a commonsense approach to women’s health and well-being, based on respect for each individual’s right to make informed, independent decisions about health, sex, and family planning. Learn more.

What is Title X Funding?

It's money that the federal government gives each state to provide family planning health care services to people who cannot otherwise afford it. The Title X funding that was allotted to Planned Parenthood was use for: cancer screenings, birth control, contraceptions, contraceptive counseling, STI screenings and STI treatment. 91% of clients of Title X centers had incomes at or below 250% of the federal poverty level.

Does Title X money cover abortions?

According to the Hyde Amendment of 1976 no federal or state funds can be used for abortions except in cases of incest, rape or saving the life of the mother.

Is this vote about abortion?

Is abortion legal?

Yes. Roe v. Wade, 410 U.S. 113 (1973).

Is Planned Parenthood an abortion clinic?

No. They are a healthcare clinic. 97% of their work is health education, reproductive health care, STI and cancer screenings, contraceptives, and family planning.

Does NH participate in fetal tissue donation programs?

No. Neither do any other states in the Planned Parenthood Northern New England region.

Is fetal tissue donation legal?

Yes.
